BasaltechSA: Pioneering Sustainable Construction
The construction and engineering industries are evolving, seeking sustainable, cost-effective solutions. Basalt fiber has emerged as a transformative material, redefining practices with its exceptional properties.

​

Corrosion is a major challenge, compromising the integrity of structures. Basalt fiber, derived from volcanic rock, offers remarkable resilience and a polymer matrix that far outperforms traditional materials in resisting corrosion.

​

Basalt Fiber Reinforced Polymer (BFRP) meshes provide durable, corrosion-resistant reinforcement for buildings, bridges, and marine structures, extending service life and reducing maintenance costs. In road construction, basalt fiber improves asphalt and concrete pavements, offering resistance to temperature fluctuations and chemical degradation.

​

BFRP is more than an alternative—it’s a superior choice for sustainability, strength, and innovation, driving a shift toward modern, eco-friendly construction.

Product Properties
Mechanical Properties
Basalt fiber boasts superior compressive and tensile strength compared to other fibers. It weighs only 25% as much as steel reinforcement but offers 2.5 times the specific tensile strength. Additionally, basalt fiber provides 20-40% better mechanical strength and puncture resistance than glass fiber. Its high tensile strength makes it ideal for high-load applications, while its lightweight nature reduces the overall weight of structures, resulting in cost savings in construction and logistics.

Environmentally Friendly Properties
​Basalt fiber is a natural and sustainable material with an energy-efficient production process. It produces 74% less carbon footprint than steel and reduces carbon dioxide emissions by 5.24 tons of coal equivalent per ton. By using BFRP, industries benefit from its superior qualities while contributing to a greener, more sustainable future.

Thermal Properties
Basalt fiber withstands temperatures ranging from -260°C to 700°C, maintaining integrity in extreme cold and heat. This makes it an excellent choice for asphalt and concrete paving applications.

Corrosion Resistance
​Unlike steel, basalt fiber is impervious to rust and corrosion. It performs exceptionally well in harsh organic, inorganic, acidic, or alkaline environments, significantly extending the lifespan of structures. Its resistance to corrosion is crucial for coastal areas and pollution control projects, including water treatment applications.

Fatigue Resistance
Basalt fiber exhibits superior fatigue resistance, ensuring sustained performance under repetitive stress and reducing the risk of failure in high-stress environments.

Electromagnetic Neutrality
Basalt fiber's excellent dielectric properties make it ideal for applications requiring electromagnetic neutrality, such as telecoms, aerospace, and medical facilities.

BasaltechSA Reinforcing Mesh

​
